THE MANIFESTO

What we believe.

This is not a startup. This is not a crypto scheme. This is not a government program waiting for approval.

This is a declaration — written by people who looked at the world and refused to accept that suffering is inevitable.

We believe the next generation deserves to inherit a world that works for them — not one they have to fight just to survive in.

01
Energy is a human right.
Without energy, there is no warmth, no food, no medicine, no communication, no future. The sun provides it freely to every corner of this planet. A world where people freeze or starve because they cannot pay an electricity bill is a world that has failed its most basic test. Energy belongs to everyone.
02
You should not have to earn the right to exist.
Every human being arrives on this planet without a choice. They did not choose to be born, or where, or to whom. The idea that survival must be earned — that you must find a buyer for your labor or go hungry — is not a law of nature. It is a law we wrote. And laws we write, we can rewrite.
03
Scarcity is a lie we tell ourselves.
The Earth produces 1.5× the food we need. The sun delivers 10,000× the energy we use. There is enough water, land, and material to house every person alive. What we call "scarcity" is almost always a failure of distribution — not a failure of production. We are not poor. We are disorganized.
04
The planet is not a resource to extract. It is a home to protect.
An energy system built on burning the Earth alive to generate profit is a system eating its own foundation. Renewable energy is not just environmentalism — it is survival strategy. When your income comes from the sun, you have every reason to keep the sky clear.
05
Unity is not weakness. Division is.
Every system designed to keep people competing with each other for scraps benefits only those who hold the scraps. Communities that cooperate — that share infrastructure, knowledge, and resources — consistently outperform those that don't. Unity is not naive. It is the most powerful force in human history.
06
The children being born today will inherit what we build.
They did not create the climate crisis. They did not design the economic systems that left billions behind. They did not vote for the decisions that built this world. But they will live — or struggle to live — in the consequences. We owe them better. Not as charity. As responsibility.
07
Small communities can change the world.
Every movement that ever changed history started with people who were told it was impossible. Abolition. Women's suffrage. Civil rights. The internet. The moon. None of it waited for unanimous consensus. It started with a few people who believed — and built — and refused to stop. That is what this is.
08
This is not the end of work. It is the end of working to survive.
People will always build, create, help, invent, teach, and connect — because those things give life meaning. What E-UBI ends is the terror: the fear that if you get sick, lose your job, or simply cannot find work, you lose everything. A floor beneath every human being is not luxury. It is the foundation of a civilization worthy of the name.

We are not waiting for permission.
We are not waiting for a perfect plan.
We are starting now — with the tools we have, the communities we live in, and the sun that rises every single morning without fail.

Join us. Build with us. The world we want already exists — we just have to choose to build it.

THE SYSTEM OF TRUTH

A blueprint for the
world that follows.

E-UBI solves survival. But survival is only the beginning. Once we guarantee that no human being suffers for the crime of being born, what comes next? This is that blueprint — a system designed not to manage scarcity, but to engineer abundance. Not to control people, but to liberate them. Not for a nation. For the species.

I
Social Foundations
Every person deserves education, a home, and a path back.
A society that allows preventable suffering is not limited by resources — it is limited by imagination. These three mandates close the gap between who we are and who we can be.
Universal High-Level Education — Not a minimum standard. An exceptional one. Every child, regardless of postcode or parentage, receives the same quality of teaching, tools, and time to become what they are capable of becoming.
Total Resolution of Homelessness — Homelessness is a logistics failure, not a character flaw. There are more empty homes than homeless people in every developed nation. We have the supply. We lack the will. This system provides both.
Rehabilitation Over Punishment — The purpose of incarceration is not suffering — it is transformation. Every facility becomes a school, a clinic, a place where damaged human beings are repaired and returned to society stronger than when they left.
II
Algorithmic Governance
No single person should hold the levers of civilization.
Every civilization that has concentrated power in one leader has, eventually, been destroyed by that decision. The solution is not better leaders. It is a better architecture — one where decisions are made by systems that cannot be bribed, lobbied, or lied to.
Decentralized Decision-Making — Power distributed across communities and regions, with no single president, party, or institution holding unilateral control. Governance becomes a network, not a hierarchy.
Non-Biased Information Systems — An open, auditable, algorithmically driven information layer surfaces objective data for every major decision. Not AI as ruler — AI as truth-teller. Humans decide. The system ensures they decide with facts, not propaganda.
Incorruptible Infrastructure — Decision-making infrastructure that is transparent by design, open-source by law, and verifiable by anyone. A government that cannot hide its reasoning is a government that cannot sustain corruption.
III
Resource Logistics
Abundance is a logistics problem. We already have the solution.
The Earth produces enough. The sun generates more than enough. The only thing standing between every human being and a life of dignity is the inefficiency of how we move, allocate, and share what we already have.
The What — Food, water, shelter, energy, healthcare, education, and connection. These are not luxuries. They are the universal inputs of a human life. The system guarantees universal access to all of them.
The How — The same information system that powers governance manages real-time resource routing. Supply, demand, waste, and surplus — tracked and rebalanced continuously. No bureaucracy. No bottlenecks. Just logistics.
Beyond the Horizon — This vision does not stop at the atmosphere. As humanity moves into space — orbital solar arrays, asteroid mining, off-world habitats — the same principles apply. Abundance that extends past the cosmos.

This is not a utopia. It is an engineering problem with known inputs, available technology, and one missing ingredient: the collective decision to build it.

The system exists. The resources exist. The only thing left to choose is whether we will.

— Max Reed

WE HAVE DONE IT BEFORE

Humanity has already done
the impossible.

Every time someone called a problem "too big to solve," someone else solved it anyway. Here is the record — the proof that coordinated human effort can change everything.

🌕
1969
We landed on the moon
Twelve years after Sputnik, 400,000 engineers and scientists put two humans on the surface of the moon — using computers less powerful than a modern calculator. When Kennedy announced the goal in 1961, nobody knew how to do it yet. They figured it out anyway.
8 years · $25B · 400,000 people · achieved
💉
1980
We eradicated smallpox
Smallpox killed 300–500 million people in the 20th century alone. In 1967, the WHO launched a global vaccination campaign. Thirteen years later, in 1980, smallpox was declared completely eradicated — the only human infectious disease ever fully eliminated. The whole world cooperated.
300M deaths stopped · 13-year campaign · 0 cases since 1977
🌤️
1987
We healed the ozone layer
Scientists discovered in 1985 that CFCs were destroying the ozone layer — threatening all life on Earth. Within two years, 197 countries signed the Montreal Protocol and banned the chemicals. The ozone layer is now recovering and is expected to fully heal by 2065. The planet fixed itself — because we stopped harming it.
197 countries · 1 treaty · ozone recovering since 2000
🦴
1994–present
Polio is almost gone
In 1988, polio paralyzed 350,000 children per year. A global campaign vaccinated billions of children across hundreds of countries. Today, wild poliovirus cases have fallen by 99.9% — only Afghanistan and Pakistan still have endemic transmission. A disease that terrified humanity for millennia is almost gone.
350,000 cases/yr → 12 cases in 2023 · 99.9% reduction
📚
1800–2024
We taught the world to read
In 1800, only 12% of the world could read and write. Today, global literacy is 87% — and rising. We took a world where knowledge was a privilege of the elite and made it the common inheritance of nearly all humanity. Not through any single act — through millions of teachers, schools, and communities, everywhere, across 200 years.
12% → 87% literate · the largest educational project in history
📉
1990–2024
Extreme poverty dropped 75%
In 1990, 36% of humanity lived in extreme poverty. By 2024, that number had fallen to under 9% — meaning over a billion people were lifted out of desperate poverty in a single generation. Child mortality fell by two thirds. Life expectancy rose in every region. The world got measurably better, on purpose.
1 billion+ people lifted · 36% → 9% · child mortality cut by 65%

None of these were inevitable. None of them were easy. All of them were called impossible by someone.

The question has never been whether we can build a better world.
The evidence says we can.

The question is whether we decide to.

THE HONEST TRUTH

You shouldn't have to work
just to exist.

This isn't radical — it's math. The planet already produces more than enough for every human alive. The problem has never been scarcity. It's always been distribution.

🌍
$105 Trillion
Global GDP in 2023
That's $13,125 per person on Earth — every man, woman and child — if it were split equally. We do not have a production problem. We have a distribution problem.
☀️
173,000 TW
Solar energy hitting Earth every second
All of humanity uses 18 TW. The sun delivers nearly 10,000× more than we need — continuously, for free, for another 5 billion years. Energy scarcity is a choice.
🌾
1.5×
More food than needed is produced annually
Earth produces enough food for 10 billion people. We are 8 billion. Yet 828 million go hungry. Food insecurity is a logistics and economics problem — not a farming problem.
🤖
85 Million
Jobs displaced by automation by 2025 (WEF)
The World Economic Forum estimates automation will displace 85 million jobs by 2025 — while only creating 97 million new ones. The net jobs are fewer, harder to access, and unevenly distributed.
⏰
15 Hours
Work week Keynes predicted by 2030 — in 1930
Economist John Maynard Keynes predicted that by 2030, technology would allow a 15-hour work week. Productivity has tripled since then. We work the same hours. The gains went up — not out.
💀
37–40%
Workers who believe their job is pointless
Anthropologist David Graeber's research found that 37–40% of workers believe their job makes no meaningful contribution to the world. His term: "bullshit jobs." We invented busyness to justify wages.
📜 A brief history of who decided you must work to survive
~10,000 BC
Hunter-gatherers worked 3–5 hours per day for food, shelter, survival. The rest was rest, community, creativity. This was the human baseline for 95% of our existence.
~1500s–1700s
The Enclosure Acts in England forcibly took shared common land from communities and gave it to private landowners. People who had farmed freely were suddenly landless — and forced into wage labor to survive.
1800s
The Industrial Revolution created 16-hour work days, child labor, and factory conditions that killed people. Working to exist wasn't natural — it was enforced by the collapse of the commons.
1938
The US Fair Labor Standards Act established the 40-hour work week as law — not as a natural truth, but as a hard-won political compromise. The 40-hour week is 86 years old. It is not physics.
Today
We produce more per hour worked than at any point in history. Automation accelerates this. The question is no longer can we afford to give people a basic income — it's why haven't we already.
⚠️ The honest part

We are not saying no one should ever work. Work gives meaning, purpose, community. What we're saying is that survival should not be the price of admission to life. No one should go homeless, hungry or unmedicated because they couldn't find a job in an economy built by machines. E-UBI doesn't solve everything — but it removes the floor from under people's feet, and replaces it with solid ground.

REAL WORLD EVIDENCE

Community energy already
works at scale.

E-UBI isn't a theory built from scratch. Every component of it — community solar, energy cooperatives, and basic income — has already been proven separately. We're combining what already works.

⚡ Energy Cooperative
REScoop — Europe
Over 1,500 energy cooperatives across Europe with more than 1 million citizen-members collectively own and operate renewable energy. Members receive dividends from energy produced. This is E-UBI without the token layer — it already exists.
Source: REScoop.eu, 2023
☀️ Community Solar
Community Solar USA — 5GW+
The US has over 5 gigawatts of community solar installed as of 2024, serving millions of households who don't own rooftops. Subscribers receive monthly bill credits directly tied to how much energy their share produces.
Source: SEIA, Wood Mackenzie, 2024
🏡 Off-Grid Cooperative
Wildpoldsried, Germany
A village of 2,600 people produces 500% more energy than it consumes through community wind, solar and biogas. They sell the surplus — and the profit funds community services. The village earns ~€4 million/year from energy alone.
Source: Rocky Mountain Institute, Village of Wildpoldsried
🌍 National Scale
Alaska Permanent Fund — 40+ Years
Since 1982, every Alaskan receives $1,000–$2,000/year as their share of state oil revenues. Alaska consistently has one of the lowest poverty rates in the US. No negative effect on workforce. It simply works.
Source: Alaska Permanent Fund Corporation
📱 Tech Layer
Power Ledger — Blockchain Energy Trading
An Australian company has already built peer-to-peer energy trading on blockchain, running live in multiple countries. Prosumers sell excess solar to neighbors directly, with every kWh tracked on-chain. The tech stack for E-UBI exists today.
Source: Power Ledger, 2023 Annual Report
🇰🇪 UBI + Energy
GiveDirectly + Solar — Kenya
GiveDirectly's long-term UBI study in Kenya found recipients invested in solar panels, livestock and small businesses — not dependency. Energy access + basic income together produced more economic activity than either alone.
Source: GiveDirectly, MIT J-PAL Research, 2022

THE CASE

Why E-UBI is
the right first step.

There are many proposals for a better world. Here's why this one is different — and honest about what it is and isn't.

🏛️
No government required
Government UBI programs require political will, tax revenue, and decades of debate. E-UBI requires neighbors, sunlight, and a smart contract. Communities can start now without waiting for policy.
⚛️
Backed by physics, not promises
Every E-UBI token represents a real kilowatt-hour that was measured, verified, and recorded. It cannot be inflated, printed, or manipulated. The sun doesn't lie. A kWh is a kWh.
📈
Scales with adoption
The more nodes that join the network, the more energy is produced, the more tokens exist, and the more valuable participation becomes. It's designed to grow. Early participants help everyone who comes after.
🔓
Open source and auditable
Every line of code, every transaction, every kWh recorded is public and auditable. No hidden fees, no middlemen taking cuts, no black boxes. This is a system you can verify yourself.
🌱
Starts as a supplement, grows into an income
We won't pretend it replaces a salary on day one. It starts as extra money — a bill paid, groceries covered. As the network grows over years, it grows toward something that genuinely reduces how many hours people must work to survive.
🤝
Builds community, not dependency
E-UBI requires communities to organize, cooperate, and maintain shared infrastructure. The process of building a node creates the social fabric that makes neighborhoods stronger — not just the income it produces.
🪞 What E-UBI honestly cannot do — yet

E-UBI will not end poverty overnight. It will not replace government welfare immediately. It will not work in places with no sun, no land, and no community organization. Token value will fluctuate with energy markets. Early networks will be small. Building takes time, money, and trust. We believe it is worth doing anyway — because every large solution started as a small honest one.
